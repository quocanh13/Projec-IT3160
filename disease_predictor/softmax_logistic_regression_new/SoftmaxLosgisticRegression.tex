    \begin{center}
        {\Large \textbf{SOFTMAX LOGISTIC REGRESSION}}
    \end{center}
    \section*{Definition:}
    - Input:
    \[
        X = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            x_1 \\
            x_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            x_n
        \end{bmatrix} (n \times 1)
    \]
    - Weight: 
    \[
        W = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            w_{1, 1} & w_{1, 2} & \hdots & w_{1, n} \\
            w_{2, 1} & w_{2, 2} & \hdots & w_{2, n} \\
            \hdots & \hdots & \hdots & \hdots \\
            w_{m, 1} & w_{m, 2} & \hdots & w_{m, n}
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times n)
    \]
    - Bias: 
    \[
        B = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            b_1 \\
            b_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            b_m
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times 1)
    \]
    - Let:
    \[
        z_i = \sum_{j = 1}^{n}w_{i, j}x_j + b_i
    \]
    - From that, we obtain matrix form:
    \[
        Z = WX + B
    \]
    - Softmax Function:
    \[
        \sigma_i = \frac{e^{z_i}}{\sum_{k = 1}^{m}e^{z_k}}
    \]
    - Label:
    \[
        Y = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            y_1 \\
            y_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            y_m
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times 1)
    \]
    - Probability that $y_i = 1$ given X:
    \[
        p_i = \sigma_i = \frac{e^{z_i}}{\sum_{k = 1}^{m}e^{z_k}}
    \]
    - Loss function using cross-entropy:
    \[
        L = -\sum_{i = 1}^{m}y_i\ln\left(p_i\right)
    \]
    \section*{Gradient:}  
    - As defined in the above section, we have:
    \[
        z_i = \sum_{j = 1}^{n}w_{i, j}x_j + b_i
    \]
    \[
        p_i = \frac{e^{z_i}}{\sum_{k = 1}^{m}e^{z_k}}
    \]
    \[
        L = -\sum_{i = 1}^{m}y_i\ln\left(p_i\right)
    \]
    - Derivative of $z_i$ respect to $w_{i, j}$:
    \[
        \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial w_{i, j}} = x_j
    \]
    - Derivative of $p_k$ respect to $z_i$:
    \[
        \frac{\partial p_k}{\partial z_i} = 
        \begin{cases}
            p_i(1-p_i), \ \ \ i = k \\
            -p_kp_i, \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ i \neq  k
        \end{cases}
    \]
    - Derivative of $L$ with respect to $p_k$ :
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial p_k} = 
        \frac{-y_k}{p_k}
    \]
    - Therefore, derivative of $L$ with respect to $w_{i, j}$ :
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_{i, j}} = 
        \sum_{k = 1}^{m}\left(
            \frac{\partial L}{\partial p_k}
            \frac{\partial p_k}{\partial z_i}
            \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial w_{i, j}}
        \right) =
        \left(
            \frac{-y_i}{p_i}p_i(1-p_i) + 
            \sum_{k \neq i}\left(\frac{-y_k}{p_k}(-p_kp_i)\right)
        \right) x_j
    \]
    \[
        = \left(
            -y_i(1-p_i) + \sum_{k \neq i}y_kp_i
        \right)x_j = 
        \left(
            -y_i + y_ip_i + \sum_{k \neq i}y_kp_i
        \right)x_j = 
        \left(
            -y_i + p_i\sum_{k = 1}^{m}y_k
        \right)x_j
    \]
    - In the matrix $Y$ we only have one label is 1, so $\sum_{k = 1}^{m}y_k = 1$ 
    \[
        \Rightarrow \left(
            -y_i + p_i\sum_{k = 1}^{m}y_k
        \right)x_j = (p_i - y_i)x_j
    \]
    - Conclusion:
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_{i, j}} = (p_i - y_i)x_j
    \]
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial b_i} = p_i - y_i
    \]
    \subsection*{Matrix Form:}
    - As we defined in the above section: \\
    Input:
    \[
        X = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            x_1 \\
            x_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            x_n
        \end{bmatrix} (n \times 1)
    \]
    Weight: 
    \[
        W = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            w_{1, 1} & w_{1, 2} & \hdots & w_{1, n} \\
            w_{2, 1} & w_{2, 2} & \hdots & w_{2, n} \\
            \hdots & \hdots & \hdots & \hdots \\
            w_{m, 1} & w_{m, 2} & \hdots & w_{m, n}
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times n)
    \]
    Bias: 
    \[
        B = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            b_1 \\
            b_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            b_m
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times 1)
    \]
    Label:
    \[
        Y = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            y_1 \\
            y_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            y_m
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times 1)
    \]
    We have probability matrix:
    \[
        P = \begin{bmatrix}
            p_1 \\
            p_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            p_m
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times 1)
    \]
    Thus, we obtain: 
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial w} = (P - Y)X^T = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            p_1 - y_1 \\
            p_2 - y_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            p_m - y_m
        \end{bmatrix}
        \begin{bmatrix}
            x_1 & x_2 & \hdots & x_n
        \end{bmatrix}
    \]
    \[
        = \begin{bmatrix}
            (p_1 - y_1)x_1 & (p_1 - y_1)x_2 & \hdots & (p_1 - y_1)x_n \\
            (p_2 - y_2)x_1 & (p_2 - y_2)x_2 & \hdots & (p_2 - y_2)x_n \\
            \hdots & \hdots & \hdots & \hdots \\
            (p_m - y_m)x_1 & (p_m - y_m)x_2 & \hdots & (p_m - y_m)x_n \\
        \end{bmatrix}
    \]
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial B} = P - Y
    \]
    \section*{Batch/Mini-batch Gradient Descent}
    - Input: 
    \[
        X = \begin{bmatrix}
            x_{1, 1} & x_{1, 2} & \hdots & x_{1, n} \\
            x_{2, 1} & x_{2, 2} & \hdots & x_{2, n} \\
            \hdots & \hdots & \hdots & \hdots \\
            x_{q, 1} & x_{q, 2} & \hdots & x_{q, n} \\
        \end{bmatrix} (q \times n)
    \]
    (q is the number of samples)\\
    - Weight: 
    \[
        W = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            w_{1, 1} & w_{1, 2} & \hdots & w_{1, n} \\
            w_{2, 1} & w_{2, 2} & \hdots & w_{2, n} \\
            \hdots & \hdots & \hdots & \hdots \\
            w_{m, 1} & w_{m, 2} & \hdots & w_{m, n}
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times n)
    \]
    - Bias: 
    \[
        B = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            b_1 \\
            b_2 \\
            \vdots \\
            b_m
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times 1)
    \]
    - Label:
    \[
        Y = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            y_{1, 1} & y_{1, 2} & \hdots & y_{1, m} \\
            y_{2, 1} & y_{2, 2} & \hdots & y_{2, m} \\
            \hdots & \hdots & \hdots & \hdots \\
            y_{q, 1} & y_{q, 2} & \hdots & y_{q, m}
        \end{bmatrix} (q \times m)
    \]
    - Z matrix:
    \[
        z_{s, i} = \sum_{j = 1}^{n}w_{i, j}x_{s, j} + b_i
    \]
    \[
        Z = XW^T + B^T \ \ (q \times m)
    \]
    - P matrix:
    \[
        p_{s, i} = \frac{e^{z_{s, i}}}{\sum_{k = 1}^{m}e^{z_{s, k}}} \\  
    \]
    \[
        P = \sigma(Z) \ \ (q \times m)
    \]
    - Loss function:
    \[
        C = \frac{1}{q}\sum_{k = 1}^{q}L_k
    \]
    ($L$ is the loss function defined in the above section)\\
    - We have derivative of $C$ respect to $W_{i, j}$:
    \[
        \frac{\partial C}{\partial w_{i, j}} = 
        \frac{1}{q}\sum_{s = 1}^{q}\frac{\partial L_s}{\partial w_{i, j}} = 
        \frac{1}{q}\sum_{s = 1}^{q}(p_{s, i} - y_{s, i})x_{s, j}
    \] 
    \[
        P - Y = \begin{bmatrix}
            p_{1, 1} - y_{1, 1} & p_{1, 2} - y_{1, 2} & \hdots & p_{1, m} - y_{1, m} \\
            p_{2, 1} - y_{2, 1} & p_{2, 2} - y_{2, 2} & \hdots & p_{2, m} - y_{2, m} \\
            \hdots  & \hdots  & \hdots & \hdots  \\
            p_{q, 1} - y_{q, 1} & p_{q, 2} - y_{q, 2} & \hdots & p_{q, m} - y_{q, m} \\
        \end{bmatrix} (q \times m)
    \]
    \[
        \Rightarrow (P - Y)^T = \begin{bmatrix}
            p_{1, 1} - y_{1, 1} & p_{2, 1} - y_{2, 1} & \hdots & p_{q, 1} - y_{q, 1} \\
            p_{1, 2} - y_{1, 2} & p_{2, 2} - y_{2, 2} & \hdots & p_{q, 2} - y_{q, 2} \\
            \hdots  & \hdots  & \hdots & \hdots  \\
            p_{1, m} - y_{1, m} & p_{2, m} - y_{2, m} & \hdots & p_{q, m} - y_{q, m} \\
        \end{bmatrix} (m \times q)
    \]
    Therefore:
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial W} = \frac{1}{q}(P - Y)^TX = 
        \left[\frac{1}{q}\sum_{s = 1}^{q}(p_{s, i} - y_{s, i})x_{s, j}\right] (m \times n)
    \]
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial B} =  \frac{1}{q}(P - Y)^T1^{q \times 1} 
        \ \ \ (m \times 1)
    \] 
    \section*{Class Weight}
    - Class weight of class $i$ : $c_i$ \\
    - As defined in the above section, we have:
    \[
        z_i = \sum_{j = 1}^{n}w_{i, j}x_j + b_i
    \]
    \[
        p_i = \frac{e^{z_i}}{\sum_{k = 1}^{m}e^{z_k}}
    \]
    - Loss function with class weight:
    \[
        L = -\sum_{i = 1}^{m}c_iy_i\ln\left(p_i\right)
    \]
    - Derivative of $z_i$ respect to $w_{i, j}$:
    \[
        \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial w_{i, j}} = x_j
    \]
    - Derivative of $p_k$ respect to $z_i$:
    \[
        \frac{\partial p_k}{\partial z_i} = 
        \begin{cases}
            p_i(1-p_i), \ \ \ i = k \\
            -p_kp_i, \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ i \neq  k
        \end{cases}
    \]
    - Derivative of $L$ with respect to $p_k$ :
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial p_k} = 
        \frac{-c_ky_k}{p_k}
    \]
    - Therefore, derivative of $L$ with respect to $w_{i, j}$ :
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_{i, j}} = 
        \sum_{k = 1}^{m}\left(
            \frac{\partial L}{\partial p_k}
            \frac{\partial p_k}{\partial z_i}
            \frac{\partial z_i}{\partial w_{i, j}}
        \right) =
        \left(
            \frac{-c_iy_i}{p_i}p_i(1-p_i) + 
            \sum_{k \neq i}\left(\frac{-c_ky_k}{p_k}(-p_kp_i)\right)
        \right) x_j
    \]
    \[
        = \left(
            -c_iy_i(1-p_i) + \sum_{k \neq i}c_ky_kp_i
        \right)x_j = 
        \left(
            -c_iy_i + c_iy_ip_i + \sum_{k \neq i}c_ky_kp_i
        \right)x_j = 
        \left(
            -c_iy_i + p_i\sum_{k = 1}^{m}c_ky_k
        \right)x_j
    \]
    - In the matrix $Y$ we only have one label is 1, so $\sum_{k = 1}^{m}y_k = 1$
    - Thus, $\sum_{k = 1}^{m}c_ky_k = c_t$
    ($c_t$ is the weight class of the true class for this example)
    \[
        \Rightarrow \left(
            -c_iy_i + p_i\sum_{k = 1}^{m}c_ky_k
        \right)x_j = c_t(p_i - y_i)x_j
    \]
    - Conclusion:
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_{i, j}} = c_t(p_i - y_i)x_j
    \]
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial b_i} = c_t(p_i - y_i)
    \]
    \subsection*{Batch/Mini-batch:}
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_{i, j}} = 
        \frac{1}{q}\sum_{s = 1}^{q}c_{s, t}(p_{s, i} - y_{s, i})x_{s, j}
    \] 
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial b_i} = 
        \frac{1}{q}\sum_{s = 1}^{q}c_{s, t}(p_{s, i} - y_{s, i})
    \] 
    ($c_{s,t}$ is the class weight of the true class for example $s$) \\
    - Let weight class matrix for true class:
    \[
        C = 
        \begin{bmatrix}
            c_{1, t} \\
            c_{2, t} \\
            \vdots \\
            c_{q, t}    
        \end{bmatrix} (q \times 1)
    \]
    - We obtain matrix form:
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial W} = \frac{1}{q}\left((P - Y) \odot C\right)^TX
    \]
    \[
        \frac{\partial L}{\partial B} = \frac{1}{q}(P - Y)^TC
    \]
